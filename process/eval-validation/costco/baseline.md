## 2. What management guided — and whether they keep their word

**Costco gives no financial guidance — by long-standing policy.** It does **not** provide next-quarter or full-year revenue/EPS ranges. The only forward figure it offers is rough annual **CapEx** and warehouse-opening plans (~30+ new warehouses/year). This is deliberate and decades old: by not guiding, Costco explicitly avoids the "beat-the-number" game. ([Motley Fool on no-guidance policy](https://www.fool.com/investing/2025/12/18/is-costco-cost-stock-a-buy-sell-or-hold-in-2026/))

Because there is **no company guide to score against, "keeps its word" must be measured a different way**: (a) is the recurring CapEx/expansion plan delivered, and (b) how does the reported quarter land versus the *consensus* the company implicitly lets analysts set? Both are disclosed and traceable; the company-guidance column is **"not disclosed — none issued"** for every quarter.

## 4. Plain-English read: confident or scared?

**Confident — almost defiantly so — and the posture is genuine, not a calendar artifact.**

The behavioral tells line up on the confident side:

- **Accelerating, not decelerating.** Net-sales growth ran 8% → 11.6% over the last four quarters and monthly comps are *strengthening* (+8% ex-gas/FX in May). A scared company doesn't post its cleanest top-line quarter in years.
- **Spending margin to win share, on purpose.** The one blemish — a ~$0.05 EPS miss on a 21-bps gross-margin dip — is the *direct result* of Costco cutting Kirkland Signature prices. That is an **offensive** move (press your advantage when rivals can't follow), not a defensive scramble. Management owned it as strategy; there was no externalizing of blame to "macro" or "media."
- **Stable leadership, no caretaker dynamics.** CEO Vachris in seat since Jan 2024; routine board re-election and KPMG ratification at the Jan 15, 2026 annual meeting. No vacuum, no interim placeholders.
- **The calendar is lean but not contracting.** Zero voluntary investor outreach in the post-Q3 window — but that's true in all five windows. Costco runs the *same* ritualized cadence (call + monthly sales + annual meeting) in every quarter. There's no quarter-over-quarter going-quiet, which is what a stress signal would look like. The monthly sales releases are themselves an unusually *transparent* habit — a company hiding something doesn't volunteer its sales twelve times a year.

**The one caveat to "confident":** Costco's posture is confident in a *passive* register, not an *active* one. It does **not** add events to sell a story, raise a buyback splashily, or court the sell-side — partly because it doesn't need to (the story sells itself) and partly because that's simply not its style. So you can't use "they added an analyst day" as positive evidence; you can only note the *absence of any withdrawal*. And because it gives no guidance, you cannot test "keeps its word on the full-year guide" at all — that test is **not disclosed/not applicable** here.

**Where the company's behavior disagrees with what the street believes:** The disagreement is **not** temporary-vs-structural (both management and analysts agree the business is healthy and compounding). It is **operations vs. valuation.** Costco is *behaving* like a company that has never been stronger — accelerating comps, record gas volumes, 89–92% renewal, aggressive price investment from a position of strength. The street largely *agrees on the business* (consensus Buy, targets ~+10% above spot) but is split on **price**: the fat Hold cohort and the $650–$1,315 target spread say "great company, debatable multiple," and the stock *falling on a beat* is the market venting ~50x-P/E expectations. The behavioral read and the fundamentals point the same way; the only thing that's wobbling is what investors are willing to pay for it.

**One-line posture:** **Confident and executing from a position of strength — a company pressing its advantage (cutting prices to take share) while staying true to a deliberately lean, no-guidance investor playbook; the only real tension is valuation, not fear.**
