# Costco Wholesale (COST) — Post-Q3-FY2026 Behavioral Read

**Anchor facts:**
- **Earnings date:** Costco reported fiscal Q3 2026 (12 weeks ended May 10, 2026) **after market close on Thursday, May 28, 2026**, with a 5:00 p.m. ET call. ([Costco IR press release](https://investor.costco.com/news/news-details/2026/Costco-Wholesale-Corporation-Reports-Third-Quarter-and-Year-To-Date-Operating-Results-For-Fiscal-2026/default.aspx); [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/costco-cost-q3-2026-earnings.html))
- **What was reported:** Net sales **+11.6% to $69.15B** (total revenue incl. membership fees ~$70.5B); net income **$2.19B, $4.93/diluted share, +15.2%** y/y. Worldwide comparable sales **+9.8%** (+6.6% ex-gas/FX); digitally-enabled comps **+21.5%**. Membership fee income **$1.37B, +10.7%**; worldwide renewal **89.7%** (US/Canada 92.2%). Both revenue and EPS beat consensus; the stock did **not** rally (swung to roughly flat-to-down after hours; initially down ~5% in intraday discussion). - **Current price:** **$982.35** at the June 12, 2026 close ([Morningstar](https://www.morningstar.com/stocks/xnas/cost/quote); [Macrotrends history](https://www.macrotrends.net/stocks/charts/COST/costco/stock-price-history)). 52-week range ~$844–$1,096; all-time-high close $1,094.32 on May 19, 2026 ([stockanalysis.com](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/cost/history/)).
- **Leadership:** Stable. CEO **Ron Vachris** (in seat since Jan 2024) and CFO **Gary Millerchip** both in place; no interim/caretaker situation. Vachris spoke on the Q3 call. ([Motley Fool transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/05/28/costco-cost-q3-2026-earnings-transcript/))

> **Important structural note on this name:** Costco is one of the quietest large caps on the calendar by design. It does **not** issue forward revenue or EPS guidance, and it largely does **not** press-release or attend sell-side broker conferences. Its recurring, *mandated/habitual* investor touchpoints are: the quarterly earnings call, **monthly net-sales releases** (with a pre-recorded message — not a live event), and the **January annual shareholders' meeting**. That baseline is essential to reading the calendar below — a "quiet" Costco window is normal, not a tell, unless it goes quiet *relative to its own monthly-sales rhythm*.

## 2. What management guided — and whether they keep their word

**Costco issues no formal forward guidance.** It does not publish next-quarter or full-year revenue/EPS ranges — confirmed again this quarter ("The warehouse club does **not** share a full-year outlook"). ([InsiderFinance, Q1 FY26](https://www.insiderfinance.io/news/costco-q1-2026-earnings-top-estimates)) Therefore the classic "guide vs. delivery / sandbagging vs. cutting the full year" analysis **cannot be run** — there is no company guide to grade actuals against, and there is no full-year guide trajectory of raises/cuts to trace. This absence is itself a posture signal (see Section 4) and is named here as **not disclosed by design**, not estimated.

## 4. Plain-English read: confident or scared?

**Verdict: Confident — quietly, structurally confident — to the point of indifference toward Wall Street's applause.**

The behavioral tells all point one direction:

- **It delivered, and accelerated.** Net-sales growth stepped up to +11.6% and EPS to +15.2%, the strongest of the trailing five quarters, with comps, e-commerce (+21.5%), and membership renewals (89.7% WW) all intact. A scared company does not print accelerating numbers; Costco did. ([Costco IR](https://investor.costco.com/news/news-details/2026/Costco-Wholesale-Corporation-Reports-Third-Quarter-and-Year-To-Date-Operating-Results-For-Fiscal-2026/default.aspx))
- **It kept its metronome, neither hiding nor over-selling.** The post-Q3 calendar is identical to its own five-quarter baseline: the call plus monthly sales releases (May sales already out June 3; June sales due ~July 8–9). There is **no going-dark** — the monthly cadence continued on schedule — but also **no defensive scramble** of extra analyst days or fireside chats. Costco didn't need to add outreach because nothing was broken. Crucially, that's the *opposite* of the "scared" signature, which is a beaten-down company going quiet. ([May sales](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/03/3306392/0/en/Costco-Wholesale-Corporation-Reports-May-Sales-Results.html))
- **No blame-shifting, stable leadership.** Management (CEO Vachris, CFO Millerchip) is intact; the call narrative was operational and forward-looking (record gas volumes, tariff-refund claims, ~28–35 new warehouses/year), not defensive. ([Motley Fool transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/05/28/costco-cost-q3-2026-earnings-transcript/))
- **Continued capital return on its own terms.** $1.47/quarter dividend, $603M buyback in the quarter, ~$20B cash — steady, not a panicky special action. ([Costco IR](https://investor.costco.com/news/news-details/2026/Costco-Wholesale-Corporation-Reports-Third-Quarter-and-Year-To-Date-Operating-Results-For-Fiscal-2026/default.aspx))

**The one caveat against reading this as max-confidence — the no-guidance posture.** Costco gives **zero** forward guidance and runs an unusually thin voluntary IR calendar. In a *struggling* company that would read as evasive (hiding the ball). For Costco it reads instead as **structural self-assurance**: a company so secure in its model and so consistently above the bar that it declines to play the guidance/roadshow game at all. The behavior is consistent quarter after quarter, in good prints and great ones — it's a posture, not a reaction to this quarter.

**Where the company's behavior disagrees with the street:** The split here is **company vs. stock**, not temporary vs. structural. Management behaves like everything is compounding (and the numbers agree) — yet the share price *fell* on a beat-and-accelerate quarter and sits ~10% below its May all-time high. The street, in turn, is majority-**Buy** but with **targets only modestly above the price** and a cluster of **Hold-while-raising-target** analysts. Both sides actually agree on the *business* (excellent, accelerating); they disagree only on whether ~47–53x earnings already prices all of that in. The "tell" is therefore unusual for this skill: there is **no behavioral red flag** to surface. The disconnect is a valuation debate layered on top of a confident operator, not a confident management story sitting over a broken business.

**One-line posture:** **Confidently business-as-usual** — Costco printed its strongest growth of the year, kept its minimalist calendar exactly to its own normal (no retreat, no scramble), gives no guidance because it doesn't have to, and the only argument on the tape is about the *multiple*, not the *company*.
