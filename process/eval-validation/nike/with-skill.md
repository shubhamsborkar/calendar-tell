**Move 1 — the latest window (Mar 31 - May 12, 2026).** The only investor event is the **Q3 FY26 earnings call itself (Mar 31, 2026)**. Nike's IR "Events & Presentations" calendar currently lists no conference appearances, fireside chats, or analyst events in this window — the next listed event is the **Q4 FY26 earnings call (June 30, 2026)**, which is *outside* the 42-day window ([Nike IR Events & Presentations](https://investors.nike.com/investors/news-events-and-reports/events-and-presentations/event-details/)). **The window is empty apart from the earnings call.**

**Critical baseline caveat (voluntary vs. mandatory).** Nike does **not** press-release broker-conference appearances, and its IR events page surfaces essentially only earnings calls. So its *traceable* baseline is **one mandatory event (the call) per quarter**. A raw count of "1" is therefore not, by itself, a scared signal — it is Nike's normal. Quiet, undisclosed appearances cannot be ruled out from public sources, and I name that limitation rather than paper over it.

**Reading the count.** The post-earnings window has been a flat **one traceable event (the call) every quarter for five quarters** — the latest window is exactly in line with Nike's own normal, so there is **no calendar "go-dark" relative to baseline.** Two structural points matter more than the flat count:

- **No window contains the annual meeting.** Nike's FY2025 annual shareholder meeting was held **Sept 9, 2025** ([SEC DEF 14A](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000320187/000032018725000048/nke-20250717.htm)) — *before* the Q1 FY26 report (Sept 30) and outside all five windows. So none of these counts is inflated by a calendar-mandated meeting.

During a drawdown, that is the conspicuous **non-event**, even though the headline count looks "normal."

## 2. What management guided — and whether they keep their word

**Nike's guidance style is itself a finding.** Nike does **not** issue hard next-quarter or full-year revenue/EPS *ranges*. It gives **directional, qualitative** guidance (e.g., "down low single digits," "down 2%-4%"), and as of Q3 FY26 it has **suspended full-year and long-term guidance entirely**, deferring it to a fall-2026 Investor Day ([Sporting Goods Intelligence](https://www.sgieurope.com/financial-results/nike-q3-beats-estimates-but-stock-falls-on-china-outlook/120352.article)). Precise full-year revenue/EPS guide ranges are therefore **not disclosed** — by management's own choice.

**Latest (Q3 FY26) guidance, verbatim/near-verbatim:**
- **Q4 FY26 revenue: down 2%-4%** year-over-year (vs. Wall Street consensus of roughly **+1.9%**) ([Sporting Goods Intelligence](https://www.sgieurope.com/financial-results/nike-q3-beats-estimates-but-stock-falls-on-china-outlook/120352.article)).
- **Greater China: down ~20%** in Q4 ([SGI](https://www.sgieurope.com/financial-results/nike-q3-beats-estimates-but-stock-falls-on-china-outlook/120352.article)).
- **Gross margin: down ~25-75 bps** in Q4, including ~250 bps of North America tariff drag; ~2-pt FX tailwind ([SGI](https://www.sgieurope.com/financial-results/nike-q3-beats-estimates-but-stock-falls-on-china-outlook/120352.article)).
- **Full-year FY26 / long-term:** **not disclosed — withdrawn**, to be reinstated at fall-2026 Investor Day ([SGI](https://www.sgieurope.com/financial-results/nike-q3-beats-estimates-but-stock-falls-on-china-outlook/120352.article)).

**Two patterns to surface:**

- **Near-term: Nike clears the bar it sets.** It came in **better than its own mid-teens decline guide in Q4 FY25** and **beat consensus EPS in each of the last several quarters** (Q1, Q2, Q3 FY26). This is the signature of **conservative near-term guidance / sandbagging** — the short-term guide is credible-to-low, and Nike tends to clear it. Trust the next-quarter direction.
- **The year is where credibility has eroded.** Nike has **stopped giving a full-year number altogether** and **pushed its Investor Day out**. You can't "miss" a full-year guide you've withdrawn — but withdrawing it during a turnaround, while still guiding *Q4 revenue to shrink 2-4%* and *China to fall ~20%*, is the honest read of where management's own confidence in the annual trajectory sits: high enough to beat a low quarterly bar, not high enough to put a year on paper.

## 4. Plain-English read: confident or scared?

**Posture: a confident *operator* sitting inside a scared-looking *story*. The blend leans cautious-defensive at the corporate-disclosure level, even though the team running it is acting like an owner, not a caretaker.**

What pushes toward **confident / owned:**
- **No leadership vacuum.** Elliott Hill is an installed, committed CEO with a reset team and a named strategy ("Win Now") — the opposite of interim/placeholder management ([Business Chief](https://businesschief.com/news/how-nike-leadership-shifts-aim-to-drive-win-now-strategy)).
- **It keeps the near-term word it gives.** It beat its own mid-teens decline guide in Q4 FY25 and has cleared the consensus EPS bar repeatedly (Q1-Q3 FY26) — a **sandbagging** pattern, which usually signals quiet confidence in the next print ([8-K Q3 FY26](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/320187/000032018726000026/q3fy26exhibit991er.htm)).
- **A genuine bright spot it points to:** North America growing (+3% in Q3, wholesale +11%) and the wholesale rebuild working ([TIKR](https://www.tikr.com/blog/nike-stock-q3-fy2026-revenue-flat-eps-down-35-but-north-america-is-healing)).

What pushes toward **scared / defensive:**
- **It guided revenue to *shrink* next quarter** (Q4 down 2-4% vs. street +1.9%) and **China to fall ~20%** ([SGI](https://www.sgieurope.com/financial-results/nike-q3-beats-estimates-but-stock-falls-on-china-outlook/120352.article)) — and the market punished it (-8% after hours).
- **It withdrew full-year and long-term guidance and deferred the Investor Day.** This is the central behavioral tell: the one **incremental, voluntary** investor event a confident turnaround would stage to sell the recovery is exactly the one Nike postponed. It is doing the **calendar minimum** — one earnings call per window, no extra outreach, no analyst day — at a five-year-low stock price.
- **Some externalizing of blame:** management repeatedly flags **tariffs, China macro, and "geopolitical volatility / Middle East / oil"** as swing factors ([Investing transcript](https://www.investing.com/news/transcripts/earnings-call-transcript-nike-beats-q3-2026-forecasts-stock-rises-93CH-4591828)), alongside its own execution.

**Where the company's behavior disagrees with the street:**
- **Temporary vs. structural.** Management frames FY2026 as a deliberate "transition year" — pulling unhealthy Classics inventory, cleaning the China marketplace, eating tariffs now for margin inflection in **Q2 FY2027** — i.e., *self-inflicted and fixable.* The street is partly pricing **structural** decline: Hold-heavy ratings, a Sell tail, and a wave of **downgrades/target cuts (HSBC, DA Davidson, Piper, RBC, UBS, Citi)** marking targets *down* toward the price.
- **The street disagreeing with itself.** Average targets say **+33-36% upside**; the consensus rating says **don't buy yet (Hold)**. The targets are lagging and still falling — the analysts don't trust their own numbers.
- **The quarter vs. the year.** A **likely beatable Q4 EPS bar (~$0.12 consensus)** sits *underneath* a **withdrawn full-year outlook** and a **shrinking-revenue, China-down-20% guide.** Nike will probably clear the low near-term bar again; the open question the withdrawn annual guide leaves on the table is the *shape and timing of the recovery*, not the next print.

**One-line posture:** *Near-term execution still credible and quietly sandbagged by a committed owner-operator — but the company is doing the public minimum on its investor calendar, has withdrawn its full-year guidance, and pushed out the one event that would sell the comeback, while guiding revenue to shrink and China to fall ~20%. The operator looks confident; the disclosure posture looks defensive, and the street is marking the story down faster than management will commit to a year.*
