## One-line verdict

**Cautiously confident, but quiet on the calendar.** Management raised full-year guidance after a beat and is using assertive, transformation-forward language — yet they have scheduled **zero** extra investor appearances in the post-earnings window and continue to hedge with "cautious outlook" framing. The body language is "show, don't tell": the calendar is bare, the tone is upbeat, and analysts are sitting on their hands with a Hold.

## 2. Do they keep their word? — Guidance vs. delivery track record

Target's recent history is **mixed**: solid delivery in FY2024, a credibility-denting cut mid-FY2025, then a confident raise to open FY2026.

| Period | Guidance action | Outcome / detail |
|---|---|---|
| **FY2024 (full year)** | **Delivered** | Full-year Adjusted EPS **$8.86**, within the original guidance range set at the start of the year. Q4 EPS $2.41 near the high end. Comps +0.1%, within range. |
| **Q1 FY2025** (May '25) | Set initial FY25 | Adjusted EPS guide **$7.00–$9.00** |
| **Q2 FY2025** (Aug '25) | Maintained | Reiterated ~$7.00–$9.00; results softening (Q2 EPS $2.05 vs $2.57 prior year) |
| **Q3 FY2025** (Nov '25) | **CUT** | Adjusted EPS lowered to **$7.00–$8.00** — top end trimmed ~$1.00. Wider-than-usual range signaling real uncertainty; comps −2.7% |
| **Q1 FY2026** (May '26) | **RAISED** | Now expects FY26 Adjusted EPS near the **high end of $7.50–$8.50**; FY26 net sales growth raised to ~**+4%** (from prior ~+2%) |

**Read:** They kept their word in FY2024 (landed inside the original range). They **broke the implied promise in FY2025** — the Q3 cut lopped ~$1 off the top of the range, which is the single most important mark against management's credibility. The Q1 FY2026 raise is encouraging and consistent with a beat, but note management deliberately guided to "near the high end" of an existing range rather than lifting the range itself — a hedged form of a raise. CEO Michael Fiddelke explicitly paired the raise with "we're maintaining a cautious outlook given the work we know we have in front of us." So: **directionally improving, recently reliable on the quarter, but only one quarter removed from a real cut** — credibility is being rebuilt, not yet restored.

## 3. The Q1 FY2026 quarter itself (context)

- **Adjusted EPS $1.71** vs. ~$1.46 expected — a **~17% beat**.
- **Revenue $25.4B, +6.7% YoY**, vs. ~$24.7B expected.
- GAAP EPS $1.71 vs. prior-year $2.27 (prior year included a one-time legal settlement gain), but **+32% vs. prior-year Adjusted EPS of $1.30**.
- Inventory turns improved >10% YoY; on track to open 30+ stores in 2026; 100+ remodels underway.
- **No buybacks** in the quarter (capacity expected later in the year) — a notably cautious capital-return posture for a company claiming a beat.
- **Stock fell ~4.9% pre-market** on the print despite the beat, on rising SG&A and lower operating margin.

**Tone signal:** Fiddelke's headline quote — *"We'll make more change to what we sell and how we sell it in 2026 than we've seen in a decade"* — is assertive and confident. But it is paired throughout with cautious hedging and no buybacks. Confident words, conservative actions.

## Bottom line

| Signal | Reading |
|---|---|
| Post-earnings calendar | **Quiet** — no extra appearances; next event is the Aug 19 Q2 call |
| Tone / language | **Confident** — "most change in a decade," guidance raised |
| Actions behind the tone | **Cautious** — hedged "high end" raise, no buybacks, "cautious outlook" |
| Guidance credibility | **Recovering** — delivered FY24, cut in Q3 FY25, raised Q1 FY26 |
| Analyst posture | **Hold**, avg target ~$131 vs. price ~$135 — stock already past target |

**Verdict:** Target's management is acting **cautiously confident, not scared** — they raised the outlook and are leaning into an aggressive transformation narrative — but they are doing it **quietly** (no incremental investor events) and with **conservative follow-through** (no buybacks, hedged raise, "cautious outlook" caveats). They are only one quarter removed from a real guidance cut, and Wall Street is reserving judgment with a Hold and a price target the stock has already overshot. The talk is confident; the behavior says "prove it."
