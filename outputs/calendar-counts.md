# Move 1 + 2 — The Investor-Event Calendar vs. Lululemon's Own Normal

*Part of the LULU calendar-tell run (June 15, 2026). Earnings reported June 4,
2026, quarter ended May 3, 2026. Every event and date is sourced inline; full
provenance in [`../data/sources.md`](../data/sources.md).*

## The thesis being tested

Confident companies put themselves in front of investors after a print; scared
ones go quiet. The complication for LULU: it **barely uses its investor calendar
in any quarter** — it does not press-release sell-side conference appearances, and
its IR "Events & Presentations" page lists essentially only mandatory items.

## The read

The most recent window shows **2 events — tied for the busiest** of the five. That
is **not** a confidence signal:

- The extra event is a calendar-mandated **annual stockholder meeting (June 25)**,
  which this year was a **contested proxy meeting** tied to a Chip Wilson
  cooperation agreement ([May 27, 2026 PR](https://corporate.lululemon.com/media/press-releases/2026/05-27-2026-123017044)) — forced engagement, not voluntary outreach.
- The ICR appearance in the Q3 window is an annual ritual, not a choice.

**Strip out the mandatory/habitual events (earnings call, annual meeting, ICR) and
the real signal is what's absent:** a company down ~60% over a year, with an
interim management team and a proxy fight, added **no incremental, voluntary
investor outreach** — no analyst day, no special investor update, no bonus
conference swing — to defend its story. A confident turnaround team usually
schedules an analyst/investor day to lay out a recovery plan. LULU did the legal
minimum.

**Caveat on completeness:** because LULU does not press-release bank-conference
appearances, undisclosed quiet appearances cannot be ruled out — but none surfaced
in IR listings, newswires, or financial calendars (MarketBeat, TipRanks).
